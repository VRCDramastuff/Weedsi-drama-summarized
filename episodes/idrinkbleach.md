## Common-Denominator Read

Weedsi is the collector and framer of this archive, and several structural patterns across it appear here as well. She ends relationships, tells the ex she needs space, continues cuddling them, then treats their jealousy as unprovoked control, here explicitly admitted in audio ("which I probably shouldn't have done"). She discloses feelings for Nebular to Bleach while post-breakup-cuddling him and then frames his jealousy reaction as abuse without noting the sequence is partly caused by her own disclosed ambivalence. She guilt-trips Nebular directly ("thanks to you i have absolutely no one / you couldnt just give me one day") and uses the threat of being alone as leverage across multiple conversations with different people ("answer or i block for good" to Bleach; "now im gonna be alone" to Nebular). The folder title "bleach blame shifting" is editorial packaging applied to a folder that contains Bleach's own voice expressing genuine pain about a real dynamic she partly concedes. The doxxing threat, the episode's most serious element, is documented in Bleach's own texts and is real; but the information being leveraged was disclosed by Weedsi herself to a partner she trusted, and the most inflammatory framing ("report dad to police" / "film his suicide") comes from caption titles and audio narration, not from captured message text. Weedsi, at the end of the long `I_talk_about_the_screenshots.m4a` audio, goes on to categorize this relationship alongside Glitch, "Toa Nova," a Deaf 21-year-old, and Starlight under a general "all men are wrong" framework, a framing that functions as a pre-emptive rebuttal to any pattern observation about her own role.

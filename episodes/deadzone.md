## Common-Denominator Read

Weedsi's own manifesto names four people plus a general category in one message ("glitch, otter, outerspaced, towa, and all these other fuckboi pickme bitches") and in a separate message lists "Outer, gunner, wolf, you" as people who have dragged her through the dirt, roughly seven unique named individuals across a single episode's two messages, with Outer appearing in both. This is her own unedited language, not a researcher's framing. The pattern, new staff role, investment of emotional energy, conflict, departure, retrospective re-labeling of the owner as abuser, has repeated with Glitch, Otter, Outer, and now Deadzone. Deadzone's diagnosis ("you bring drama") is not documented with specific incidents, but the screenshots in this very folder show Weedsi introducing three separate complaint threads (Silvy, Sincere, Alaxous) in a single afternoon, shifting frames when each was questioned. His "drama" complaint is corroborated by her own behavior in the record she chose to save. The relationship was mutually enmeshed, both used family language ("father figure," "you are my family"), both vented to Woldry, both escalated and then de-escalated, which makes the clean abuser/victim frame inapplicable. Weedsi's decision to detonate the conversation with a long manifesto on a day she was at her grandmother's deathbed, then spend four hours in calls negotiating her re-hire, is not consistent with someone simply trying to escape abuse. The 2-hour voice call (12:33 to 2:33 PM) is an evidentiary black box, no recording exists, so neither her abuse claims about that call nor his implicit denial can be verified either way.
